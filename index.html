FOX 86 Allentown

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.

Asher Bishop, 03/05/2026, 8:45 AM

An image of a brown fox. (Source: Unsplash)

A 1-in-a-million situation happened in Scranton, PA on Tuesday. A security camera caught a moment where a woman was leaving her house, when a fox leaps in through the front door, hops over her sleeping dog, and exits again.

"I couldn't process what had happened," The woman said, "It was like something out of a cartoon."

The incident is related to a string of events involving foxes in the greater Scranton area. Earlier this month, near Waverly, A pack of foxes entered a farm, snacked on some of the hens and their eggs, and even started riding the horses. This invasion caused an estimated $150,000 dollars in damages.

Another pack of foxes interfered with an ongoing criminal investigation in the Wilkes-Barre town square. "This is unbelievable," the town sheriff said to us in an interview, "Some of the evidence is ruined."

Mayor Hubert Jenkins has decided that the Scranton Town Council and Animal Control units have a briefing on this issue, he announced this afternoon. "The situation is unprecented," the mayor commented, "but we have to double down on these out-of-control foxes."
